# 📊 Wes Anderson Desk Variations Guide

These variations expand your visual B-roll toolkit. They are shot with identical color tones matching your app, allowing you to edit with quick, geometric cuts—whip-panning or cutting between different angles of the same desk space to keep the video visually dynamic while maintaining narrative cohesion.

---

## 📁 Variations Directory Overview

The files are located in the `variations/` directory:

*   **`variations/wes_variation_oblique.png` (High-Angle Close-up):**
    *   *Visual:* Focuses closely on the vintage reel-to-reel tape recorder. Keyboard, tea cup, and notebook are softly blurred in the background.
    *   *Editing Use:* Perfect when discussing "local atomic memory storage" or "token logs," drawing focus directly onto the spinning reels.
*   **`variations/wes_variation_left.png` (Panned Left Flat-lay):**
    *   *Visual:* The camera is shifted to the left, centering the tape recorder, with only the left edges of the keyboard and tea mug visible.
    *   *Editing Use:* Excellent B-roll slide when talking about logging processes, permissions, or system resources.
*   **`variations/wes_variation_right.png` (Panned Right Flat-lay):**
    *   *Visual:* Centered on the leather notebook and fountain pen, with a warm vintage desk lamp glow casting from the top right.
    *   *Editing Use:* Best for when discussing "defining user intent," "planning," or "harness engineering documentation."
*   **`variations/wes_variation_front.png` (Low-Angle straight-on):**
    *   *Visual:* Looking straight across the keyboard towards the CRT screen and the tape deck, with a symmetrical small succulent plant.
    *   *Editing Use:* Great for a "Direct-to-CRT" zoom, or when transitioning between talking-head shots and full screen walkthroughs.

## 💡 How to Cut Symmetrical Scenes (Wes Anderson Style)
1.  **Use 90-degree Snap Whip-pans:** When transitioning from the panned-left image (`wes_variation_left.png`) to the panned-right image (`wes_variation_right.png`), use a very fast horizontal slide transition (10 frames) with motion blur. This mimics a physical camera whipping across the desk.
2.  **Maintain Symmetrical Eyelines:** Symmetrical setups work because they keep the center of interest balanced. When cutting from the wide desk to the oblique tape close-up, keep the focus point perfectly aligned with one of your horizontal grid thirds.
3.  **Sync SFX with Cuts:** Every snap whip-pan or cut should be accompanied by a mechanical keyboard clack, tape clunk, or a soft slide 'whoosh' to make the editing feel satisfying and high-production.
